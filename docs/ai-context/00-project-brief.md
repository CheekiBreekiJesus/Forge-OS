# 00 Project Brief

## 1. What the Project Is

ForgeOS is a web-based Industrial Operating System for manufacturing companies.

It is intended to combine several operational tools into one platform:
- ERP-style business management
- Warehouse management
- Production management
- Maintenance management
- CRM
- Marketing tools
- Website/content management
- Internal newsletters
- AI-assisted workflows

## 2. Who It Is For

Primary first user:
- JH Gomes, a small manufacturing and cup-printing business in Portugal.

Target future users:
- Plastic injection companies
- Mold manufacturers
- CNC machining workshops
- Packaging manufacturers
- Automation integrators
- Industrial assembly operations
- Small and medium manufacturing businesses

## 3. Problem It Solves

Small manufacturing companies often operate with fragmented systems:
- Excel sheets
- Paper notes
- Phone-based orders
- Manual production tracking
- Disconnected stock management
- No centralized documentation
- Weak digital marketing systems
- Limited automation

ForgeOS should centralize daily operations in one system.

## 4. Core Value Proposition

“One Operating System for the Modern Factory.”

ForgeOS gives small and medium manufacturers a single platform to manage:
- Customers
- Orders
- Production
- Machines
- Inventory
- Maintenance
- Documentation
- Marketing
- AI-assisted administrative and operational work

## 5. Current Development Stage

Current stage: prototype / early MVP planning.

Known:
- Product vision is defined.
- First deployment target is defined.
- Initial module ideas are defined.
- Localization requirements are defined.

Not yet defined:
- Final database
- Authentication provider
- Hosting
- Production architecture
- Exact MVP feature list
- Full UI implementation

## 6. Main Constraints

- Must support multi-tenant SaaS architecture from the beginning.
- Backend/internal code must remain in English.
- Frontend must support European Portuguese and English.
- JH Gomes must be treated as first tenant, not as the only possible company.
- System should be practical for small manufacturing teams.
- Initial workflows should not require heavy enterprise complexity.
- Must avoid exposing private customer or business data.
- Must be usable by non-technical factory users.

## 7. Non-Goals

Not initial goals:
- Full enterprise ERP replacement on day one
- Complex humanoid robot integration
- Fully autonomous AI decision-making
- Advanced financial/accounting suite
- Deep machine-control integration
- Large-scale MES complexity
- Native mobile app unless later justified
- Spanish localization in the first MVP unless simple to prepare structurally
